Designing for humans, not users.

Hi I'm Tom, a compassionate, intuitive thinker who likes to tell stories and solve problems in imaginative and creative ways.

UX designer and recovering software engineer — 3 years sober.

I research, design, prototype, test, deliver and measure

I discover, to define the problem

I use a process of thoughtful questioning, perceptive observation, and diligent research to understand business goals, uncover user needs, and expose opportunities.

I gather insight by conducting interviews, reviewing usability, analysing competitors, and studying user behaviour.

Insight enables me to empathise with perspectives and embrace constraints so I can focus on high value problems that cause real pain.

I design with paper & people

I'll sit down with my Moleskine, pens, and and Promarkers to sketch interactions and flows, taking into account heuristics, context of use and user needs.

Low fidelity allows me the creative freedom to iterate quickly without becoming attached to solutions or distracted by digital tools.

I prototype with code

I sketch in code to create realistic, responsive, and interactive prototypes.

Using code enables me to focus on the technical considerations of a design: responsive flow, document hierarchy, performance, progressive enhancement, usability, and accessability.

By combining logic-less JS templating with dummy data I can quickly test an interface against edge cases and transient or empty states.

I test with users

Ideally, prototypes are tested with real users in real situations. However, I'm partial to guerrilla testing when deadlines are tight. Testing validates design decisions, evaluates usability, and uncovers subtleties not exposed during discovery.

Prototypes have a primary purpose; is my understanding and design thinking right or wrong?

I develop with love

I'm borderline obsessive about tooling, CSS architecture, semantics, accessability, and design systems.

I have over 15 years' experience collaborating with teams of all sizes. No matter the environment one aspect of my workflow has remained consistent - The use of pattern-led development.

Accessabile, mobile first, mustard cutting, progressively enhanced awsomeness.

I continue to learn when it's live

By establishing, and benchmarking, key metrics upfront we can gauge success by measuring how users engage across physical and digital environments.

Combining big data and thick (qualitative) data allows us to understand both the ‘what’ and ‘why’ of user behaviour.
